Types of ceramics

Porcelain: is a ceramic material made by heating substances

Stoneware: is a clay that when fired to maturity becomes a sturdy

Earthenware: It is the most commonly found clay in nature, and is the raw material used to make tiles, bricks, and most pottery products.

Potters wheels

Motorized:

Are electric Wheels that can be powered up with the flick of a switch

Kick wheel (non-motorized):

Require you to use your foot to kick the concrete fly-wheel into motion to make the wheel spin.

What does the kiln do to the piece?

turns raw clay into ceramic through high-temperature heating.

How does the firing work?

Clay often goes through two types of firing - bisque firing and glaze firing.

ceramic glazes

Process of glazing:

mixing your glazes, applying the glaze to bisque-ware, letting it dry, then finally loading it into the kiln for the glaze firing.

Glazes types:

low-fire pottery glazes, mid-fire pottery glazes, and high-fire pottery glazes
